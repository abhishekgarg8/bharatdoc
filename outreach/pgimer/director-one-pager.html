Proposed 30-day controlled pilot for PGIMER, Chandigarh

Doctor-reviewed AI documentation for high-volume OPDs

BharatDoc helps doctors record a consultation, review an AI-drafted clinical note, edit it, and save a PGIMER-approved patient-identifier PDF without changing PGIMER's HIS workflow.

Why this matters at PGIMER

PGIMER's OPDs combine high patient volume, teaching responsibilities, multilingual consultations, and heavy documentation pressure. BharatDoc is designed to measure whether reviewed notes can be completed with less documentation effort while keeping the doctor fully accountable for the final clinical record.

The pilot is documentation support only: no autonomous diagnosis, no prescribing, and no EHR writeback.

What the pilot tests

  1. Can reviewed notes be completed with less documentation effort?
  2. Do transcripts handle Indian OPD speech well enough?
  3. Are AI summaries clinically acceptable after doctor review and correction?
  4. Can PGIMER evaluate the workflow without system integration risk?
30 days Controlled pilot, not public rollout
10-20 Doctors/residents across 1-3 OPD teams
100-300 Consultations measured for quality and workflow impact

Doctor workflow

  1. Open BharatDoc on phone or browser.
  2. Record consultation audio with consent process defined by PGIMER.
  3. Generate transcript and structured clinical summary.
  4. Doctor reviews and edits the note.
  5. Save a PGIMER-approved patient-identifier PDF for later lookup.

Governance and approvals

  • Doctor remains final reviewer before saving.
  • AI-assisted disclaimer appears on generated PDFs.
  • No integration with PGIMER HIS during pilot.
  • Role-based clinic access and owner approval.
  • Pilot launch subject to PGIMER IEC/IRB, IT/security, data-protection, department, and patient-consent approvals.

Success measures

  • Median time from recording stop to reviewed note.
  • Transcription success rate by device and language.
  • Doctor edit rate by summary section.
  • PDFs generated per participating doctor.
  • Doctor satisfaction and clinical-quality feedback.

Request

We request a 20-minute meeting with the Director's nominated clinical, IT/security, legal/data-protection, IEC/ethics, and administration representatives to review feasibility and approvals for a controlled OPD pilot.

Suggested next step: live 5-minute demo using a deidentified sample consultation.
